# Park-Themed Blender Asset Ideas

Use this as a backlog for future Bouneurmaum National Park environment assets. These should reinforce the rural national-park setting from [`docs/setting.md`](../setting.md), add gameplay-readable cover or landmarks, and avoid drifting into city, industrial, sci-fi, or heavy military themes.

## Best Next Blender Candidates

1. **Trailhead kiosk**
   - Large wooden map board with park notices, trail icons, and dark-brown/cream park-service colors.
   - Strong landmark from drone height.
   - Useful near spawn, road junctions, or objective-adjacent clearings.

2. **Split-rail fence kit** — *implemented, see [briefs/park_fence_set.md](briefs/park_fence_set.md)*
   - Shipped: straight tileable bay, heavy corner/end post, and a working swing gate
     (`FenceSplitRail`, `FenceSplitRailCorner`, `FenceSplitRailGate`).
   - Helps shape paths without using glowing blockout line strips.
   - Can provide partial visual cover while keeping movement readable.
   - Open follow-ups: broken and leaning variants if more dressing is wanted.

3. **Fallen log cover set**
   - Straight log, angled log, hollow log, mossy log, and branchy variant.
   - Natural low cover that fits the park better than generic barricades.
   - Good for forest edges and road approaches.

4. **Small wooden footbridge**
   - Short bridge over a ditch, dry creek, or shallow ravine.
   - Adds vertical silhouette and a clear traversal landmark.
   - Can support above/below map identity without feeling artificial.

5. **Ranger / service-road gate**
   - Weathered metal or timber barrier arm with park-service signage.
   - Strong road-control landmark.
   - Works as cover, route framing, or a transition into a staging area.

6. **Trail marker sign cluster**
   - Directional arrows for "Ranger Station", "Overlook", "Camp Loop", "Service Road", and "Visitor Area".
   - Small but high-value for identity and orientation.
   - Can be reused around the map with different sign plates.

7. **Covered picnic shelter**
   - Open-sided timber shelter with posts, roof, picnic tables, and maybe a grill/fire ring nearby.
   - Large readable structure that still fits a national park.
   - Useful as a contestable midpoint or objective landmark.

## Gameplay Props

- Big stump clusters for low cover.
- Mossy boulder clusters with dirt buildup at the base.
- Timber trail stairs built into a slope.
- Gravel parking pull-off with wheel ruts and timber parking stops.
- Rope-and-post trail railing.
- Rocky overlook ledge pieces.
- Drainage culvert near a service road.
- Timber retaining wall segments for switchbacks.
- Park bench variants: intact, tilted, and broken slat.
- Picnic table variants: intact, weathered, and overturned.

## Park Identity Props

- Park rules board with weathered glass or plexiglass.
- Numbered campsite posts.
- Bear-proof trash cans or park bins.
- Fire ring with ash, stones, and old logs.
- Drinking fountain or water spigot post.
- Small restroom or vault toilet building.
- Visitor information board.
- Park-service utility shed.
- Carved wooden mascot/statue near an entrance or visitor area.
- Flagpole or park-service banner.

## Drone-Readable Landmarks

- Circular picnic pavilion.
- Ranger lookout mini-tower.
- Wooden observation platform.
- Visitor-center roof sign or roof silhouette.
- Firewatch-style platform with railings.
- Water-tank utility shed paired with the existing water tower.
- Tall trail map kiosk.
- Service-road gate with broad sign panel.

## Incident Dressing

Keep these sparse so the map still reads as a park where an incident happened, not a military base.

- Folding tables for the command tent area.
- Cable reels and battery cases.
- Rugged drone launch crate.
- Cones and sawhorses for a closed trail.
- Incident-response notice board.
- Portable generator and fuel can.
- Field antenna tripod.
- Stacked supply crates under a tarp.

## Nature Dressing

- Fern clumps and underbrush patches.
- Dead branch piles.
- Pine needle ground mats.
- Muted wildflower patches.
- Small sapling clusters.
- Roots breaking through trail dirt.
- Moss sheets for rocks and logs.
- Dry creek stones and muddy banks.

## Theme Guardrails

- Prefer weathered timber, bark, dirt, gravel, moss, faded ranger green, and dark-brown/cream signage.
- Reserve bright colors for gameplay readability and objective signaling.
- Do not add glowing route, approach, danger, breach, launch-pad, or other line-strip markers to the playable level.
- Avoid dense urban, industrial, sci-fi, military-base, horror, or post-apocalyptic styling.
- When an asset becomes a real production target, create a focused asset brief under `docs/assets/briefs/` before detailed Blender work.
